Questions

Frequently asked

Does this cost me anything?
No. We're paid a commission by the landlord when a lease closes — standard practice in commercial real estate. You never pay us a fee for representation.
What's a "2nd generation" restaurant space?
A space that was previously a restaurant, so the hood system, grease trap, walk-in, and other kitchen infrastructure are often already in place. That typically means a faster, cheaper build-out compared to vanilla shell space.
Do you also work with landlords?
No — we represent restaurant operators exclusively. We never take the landlord's side of a deal.
I don't have funding lined up yet. Can you still help?
Yes. Traditional SBA financing is commonly used for restaurant acquisitions and build-outs, though it typically requires underwriting and takes time. Depending on your situation, we can also introduce you to lending partners offering faster working-capital options. We don't guarantee approval or timelines — that's determined by the lender — but we can point you to the right conversation.
I'm already working with a broker. Can I still reach out?
Yes — let us know on the form. If you have an exclusive agreement with another broker, we'll respect that and won't step on it.
What happens after I submit the form?
Sonny personally reviews every submission and follows up within 1 business day to talk through your concept, budget, and timeline before any space is shared.
Do you help after I sign the lease too?
Yes. Beyond the lease, we connect operators with trusted independent professionals — lenders, POS providers, contractors, and others — as you move toward opening. Stonewood does not provide legal, financial, insurance, or lending services directly; we make introductions as a courtesy.
